Our Pediatric Cardiology Services

We offer a comprehensive range of diagnostic and therapeutic services for heart conditions affecting infants, children, and adolescents.

Advanced Cardiac Diagnostics

Our state-of-the-art diagnostic capabilities allow us to precisely identify and evaluate pediatric heart conditions, enabling targeted treatment plans.

Pediatric Echocardiography (2D, Doppler)

A painless, non-invasive ultrasound test that creates detailed images of your child's heart structure and function. It helps evaluate heart chambers, valves, walls, blood vessels, and blood flow patterns to diagnose various heart conditions.

Fetal Echocardiography

A specialized ultrasound that examines the heart of an unborn baby, typically performed during the second trimester of pregnancy. This test can detect congenital heart defects before birth, allowing for early planning and intervention when needed.

Electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG)

A quick, painless test that records the electrical activity of your child's heart. It helps detect irregular heartbeats (arrhythmias), heart chamber enlargement, and other cardiac conditions.

Holter Monitoring

A portable device your child wears for 24-48 hours that continuously records heart activity during normal daily activities and sleep. It helps identify irregular heart rhythms that may not be detected during a regular office visit.

Trans-Esophageal Echo (TEE)

An advanced diagnostic procedure that provides detailed images of the heart's structure and function from inside the esophagus. Used in specific cases where standard echocardiography may not provide sufficient detail.

Exercise Stress Testing

For older children and adolescents, this test evaluates how the heart performs during physical activity, helping to diagnose exercise-induced cardiac symptoms or assess exercise capacity in those with known heart conditions.

Minimally Invasive & Non-Surgical Interventions

Dr. Khatri specializes in advanced catheter-based interventions that can repair certain heart defects without open-heart surgery, reducing recovery time and complications.

Device Closure (ASD, VSD, PDA)

A minimally invasive procedure to close holes between heart chambers (ASD, VSD) or abnormal connections between blood vessels (PDA) using specialized devices inserted through catheters. These procedures typically require only a small incision in the groin and allow for rapid recovery.

Balloon Valvuloplasty / Angioplasty

A technique that uses a balloon-tipped catheter to open narrowed heart valves (valvuloplasty) or blood vessels (angioplasty). The balloon is inflated to stretch the narrowed area, improving blood flow without surgical intervention.

Coil Embolization

A procedure to close abnormal blood vessels or unwanted vascular connections using tiny coils delivered through catheters. The coils cause blood to clot, effectively sealing off the abnormal vessel.

Neonatal Interventions

Specialized interventional procedures adapted for newborns with critical heart conditions, allowing for life-saving treatments in the smallest and most vulnerable patients.

Dr. Khatri has particular expertise in non-surgical device closures, having successfully performed these procedures on hundreds of children with excellent outcomes.

Consultation and Disease Management

We provide comprehensive evaluation and ongoing management for children with all types of heart conditions.

Evaluation of Heart Murmurs

Expert assessment of heart murmurs to differentiate innocent (harmless) murmurs from those indicating structural heart problems, often alleviating parental anxiety with accurate diagnosis.

Management of Congenital Heart Defects

Comprehensive care for children born with heart abnormalities, including pre-operative evaluation, non-surgical interventions when appropriate, and post-operative follow-up care.

Treatment of Acquired Heart Diseases

Diagnosis and management of conditions that develop after birth, such as Kawasaki disease, myocarditis, rheumatic heart disease, and cardiomyopathy.

Arrhythmia Management

Evaluation and treatment of irregular heart rhythms in children, including medical management and coordination with electrophysiology specialists when needed.

Preventive Cardiology Counseling

Guidance on heart-healthy lifestyles, risk factor management, and preventive care for children at risk of developing heart disease, including those with family history or medical conditions that affect heart health.
